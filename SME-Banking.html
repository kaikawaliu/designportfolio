    My Role: ​I am the product designer for the full transfer product line, responsible for related UX/UI Design works, User Research, and overall Design Standards.

1.​ SME transfer

Handle payments efficiently and securely with DBS's comprehensive suite of domestic and cross-border transfers

Problem / Challenge

Complex Form

Compare with competitor's transfer flow, existing user journey is too complex with unclear payment types and unecessary optional input fields.

Low FX Utilization

FX booking experience is too complex for those who are not professional dealers, and not enough guide and alert to remind FX utilization. FX rate is not competitive compared with other platform.

Lacking of transparency

Always with hidden fees that only shown  on e-statement, no tracking feature to check transfer states.

Business Goal

1. Offer lower rates to retain customers with DBS

2. To grow FX revenue for DBS

3. To grow Volumes and thus fee revenue to DBS

4. To bring back customer who are either using MSB or other banks for TT due to competitive pricing (Fees + FX)

5. To win new client mandates

6. To enhance customer experience.  

User Journey

Break down the user journey into five stages: Trigger, Awareness, Consideration, Conversion and Advocacy & Repeat Behaviour

User Interview

At UX design stage, quantitative research was done to prove hypothesis and understand user needs.

User Testing

Qualitative research using figma prototype, monitored user behaviour in order to improve the product
